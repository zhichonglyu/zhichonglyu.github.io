Abstract: We examine the evolution of economics research using a machine-learning-based classification of publications into fields and styles. The changing field distribution of publications would not seem to favor empirical papers. But economics' empirical shift is a within-field phenomenon; even fields that traditionally emphasize theory have gotten more empirical. Empirical work has also come to be more cited than theoretical work. The citation shift is sharpened when citations are weighted by journal importance. Regression analyses of citations per paper show empirical publications reaching citation parity with theoretical publications around 2000. Within fields and journals, however, empirical work is now cited more.

导读

经济学研究越来越趋向于实证化（Hamermesh，2013），本文深入研究了这一变化，揭示其主要体现在经济学领域内的研究风格变化上，并非跨领域的转变。基于机器学习模型，作者根据内容将经济学论文进行领域和风格分类（Angrist，2017），研究出版风格的演变是否伴随着影响力的改变。

Introduction

Economics research has become increasingly empirical (Hamermesh, 2013). This paper investigates this shift, revealing that it is primarily a within-field change in research style rather than a cross-field transition. Using machine learning models, the authors classify economics papers by field and style based on content (Angrist, 2017), examining whether the evolution of publication styles is accompanied by changes in scholarly impact.

领域与风格分类

本文使用Web of Science和EconLit数据库中经济学期刊的相关数据，分析论文发表量和引用率。

Field and Style Classification

This paper uses data from economics journals indexed in Web of Science and EconLit to analyze publication volumes and citation rates.

领域分类

领域分类基于JEL代码、标题和关键词、论文及其引用文献的领域等四个信息来源。参考Ellison（2002）将JEL分类映射到17个领域的做法，作者手工将论文分配到相应领域，并将其作为训练数据来源。机器学习算法为每篇论文生成初始领域分类，聚类算法利用论文的初始分类及其所引用论文的初始分类，为每篇论文分配最终领域（详细说明可参考在线附录，https://www.aeaweb.org/content/file?id=4416 ）。

根据论文内容，作者将其聚类为微观经济学、宏观经济学、计量经济学、公共财政、劳动经济学、国际经济学、金融学、产业组织，发展经济学和综合经济学等十个领域。其中，综合经济学是较小领域的综合，包括经济史、环境经济学、实验经济学、法律与经济学、政治经济学和城市经济学等。

Field Classification

Field classification draws on four information sources: JEL codes, titles and keywords, paper fields, and the fields of cited references. Following Ellison (2002), who mapped JEL codes to 17 fields, the authors manually assigned papers to corresponding fields as training data. A machine learning algorithm generates an initial field classification for each paper, and a clustering algorithm then assigns a final field using both the paper's initial classification and the initial classifications of its cited references (see the online appendix at https://www.aeaweb.org/content/file?id=4416 for details).

Based on paper content, the authors cluster papers into ten fields: microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, public finance, labor economics, international economics, finance, industrial organization, development economics, and miscellaneous economics. The miscellaneous category aggregates smaller fields including economic history, environmental economics, experimental economics, law and economics, political economy, and urban economics.

风格分类

本文根据论文内容将其划分为三种研究风格，包括理论研究、实证研究和计量经济学风格。首先，计量经济学领域分类的论文被归类为计量经济学风格。然后，将使用数据估计经济上有意义参数的论文标记为实证研究。最后，将其余论文分类为理论研究。

本文通过机器学习算法进行风格分类，训练集包含5950篇手工分类的论文，但这一做法过度分类了顶级期刊。其中，训练集包括：

  • （1）Ellison（2002）最初分类的文章，1971年至1998年前六本经济学期刊，共计1507篇论文；
  • （2）来自AER、JPE和Econometrica的论文：

- AER1992年至2004年的436篇论文；

- Econometrica从1998年至2013年的822篇论文；

- JPE从1987年至2014年的933篇论文；

  • （3）本文采用的列表期刊，从1980年至1989年发表的文章中随机选择的15篇论文，共计1080篇论文；
  • （4） 从1990年至1999年、2000年至2013年AER引用量排名前20的期刊中，每十年随机选择每个期刊的15篇论文；每十年随机选择其他期刊的5篇论文，总计1172篇文章。

Style Classification

This paper classifies papers into three research styles based on content: theoretical, empirical, and econometric. First, papers classified in the econometrics field are assigned the econometric style. Next, papers that use data to estimate economically meaningful parameters are labeled as empirical. The remaining papers are classified as theoretical.

Style classification is performed via a machine learning algorithm trained on 5,950 manually classified papers, though this procedure over-samples top journals. The training set comprises:

  • (1) Articles originally classified by Ellison (2002) from the top six economics journals, 1971--1998, totaling 1,507 papers;
  • (2) Papers from AER, JPE, and Econometrica:

- 436 AER papers from 1992--2004;

- 822 Econometrica papers from 1998--2013;

- 933 JPE papers from 1987--2014;

  • (3) 15 randomly selected papers per journal from articles published between 1980 and 1989 in the journals used in this study, totaling 1,080 papers;
  • (4) From the top 20 journals by AER citation count in 1990--1999 and 2000--2013, 15 randomly selected papers per journal per decade; 5 randomly selected papers from other journals per decade, totaling 1,172 articles.

Journal Weights

Scholars pay greater attention to papers in prestigious, widely cited general-interest journals. To capture this journal hierarchy, the paper introduces importance weights denoted $w_{j}^{t}$.

$w_{j}^{t}$ reflects the extent to which journal $j$ (from the paper's journal list) is cited by a weighted combination of the top six journals. The Google PageRank algorithm is first applied to the citation matrix (see the online appendix at https://www.aeaweb.org/content/file?id=4416 for details). This matrix yields within-journal citation shares $μ_k^t$, from which a weighted average of citation shares is computed. The final measure is a five-year moving average.
