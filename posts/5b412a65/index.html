Journal | MS | ChatGPT for textual analysis? How to use generative LLMs in accounting research期刊 | MS | ChatGPT能用于文本分析吗？如何在会计研究中使用生成式大语言模型

Created2025-01-18|Updated2026-03-06|Journal
|Word Count:574|Reading Time:2mins|Post Views:

封面来源：ChatGPT-4 DALL·E
Cover Source: ChatGPT-4 DALL·E
摘要：生成式大语言模型（GLLMs），如OpenAI的ChatGPT和GPT-4，正成为会计研究中文本分析任务的强大工具。GLLMs不仅能解决非生成式方法可完成的所有文本分析任务，还能处理以往只能通过人工编码完成的任务。尽管GLLMs功能强大，但也存在局限性，并带来了需要谨慎应对的新挑战。本文重点介绍了GLLMs在会计研究中的应用，并与现有方法进行了比较。文章还提供了一个有效使用GLLMs的框架，涵盖模型选择、提示工程和确保构念效度等关键问题。在案例研究中，作者通过检测财报电话会议中的回避性回答（一项传统上难以自动化的任务）展示了GLLMs的能力。新的GPT方法达到了96%的准确率，并将回避性回答的错误率相对于现有的Gow et al. (2021)方法降低了70%。最后，文章讨论了使用GLLMs时应关注的偏差、可复制性和数据共享问题。总体而言，本文为研究者、审稿人和编辑提供了有效使用和评估GLLMs进行学术研究的知识与工具。
Abstract: Generative large language models (GLLMs), such as ChatGPT and GPT-4 by OpenAI, are emerging as powerful tools for textual analysis in accounting research. GLLMs can solve any textual analysis task solvable using nongenerative methods as well as tasks previously only solvable using human coding. While powerful, GLLMs also come with limitations and new challenges requiring care and due diligence. This paper highlights GLLM applications for accounting research and compares them with existing methods. It provides a framework for effectively using GLLMs, addressing key considerations such as model selection, prompt engineering, and construct validity. In a case study, the author demonstrates GLLM capabilities by detecting nonanswers in earnings conference calls, a traditionally challenging task to automate. The new GPT method achieves 96% accuracy and reduces the nonanswer error rate by 70% relative to the Gow et al. (2021) method. Finally, the paper discusses the importance of addressing bias, replicability, and data sharing concerns when using GLLMs. Overall, this paper equips researchers, reviewers, and editors with the knowledge and tools to effectively use and evaluate GLLMs for academic research.
