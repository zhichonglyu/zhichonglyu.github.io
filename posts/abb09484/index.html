Abstract: Growing AI readership (proxied for by machine downloads and ownership by AI-equipped investors) motivates firms to prepare filings friendlier to machine processing and to mitigate linguistic tones that are unfavorably perceived by algorithms. Loughran and McDonald (2011) and BERT available since 2018 serve as event studies supporting attribution of the decrease in the measured negative sentiment to increased machine readership. This relationship is stronger among firms with higher benefits to (e.g., external financing needs) or lower cost (e.g., litigation risk) of sentiment management. This is the first study exploring the feedback effect on corporate disclosure in response to technology.

摘要：随着人工智能（AI）读者数量的增加（以机器下载量和拥有AI设备的投资者为代表），企业逐渐调整其披露文件，使其更便于机器处理，同时降低算法不利解读的语言语调。通过Loughran and McDonald（2011）以及自2018年开始使用的BERT事件研究，证实了机器读者增加导致负面情绪减少的现象。这一关系在具有较高外部融资需求或较低情绪管理成本（如诉讼风险）的企业中表现得更为明显。这是首项探讨企业披露受技术反馈影响的研究。
Summary: Growing AI readership—proxied by machine downloads and ownership by AI-equipped investors—motivates firms to make filings more machine-friendly and to mitigate linguistic tones unfavorably perceived by algorithms. Event studies around Loughran and McDonald (2011) and BERT (available since 2018) confirm that increased machine readership drives the decline in measured negative sentiment. This relationship is stronger among firms with greater external financing needs or lower litigation risk. This is the first study to explore the feedback effect of technology on corporate disclosure.

引言

Introduction

年报（以及其他监管文件）不仅仅是上市公司的法律要求；它为企业提供了一个机会，用以传达财务健康状况、推广企业文化和品牌，并与广泛的利益相关者互动。这些读者如何处理这些丰富的信息，将显著影响他们对企业的认知，进而影响他们参与企业的活动。企业越来越意识到，其强制性和自愿性披露的目标受众不再仅限于人类分析师和投资者。大量的股票买卖交易是由使用机器学习工具和自然语言处理工具包处理信息的机器人和算法给出的建议所驱动的。1

年报和披露的多重功能（年报不仅仅是法律要求，它还是企业传达财务健康、推广品牌文化、与利益相关者沟通的机会）→→→读者的认知影响（读者如何处理这些信息，将直接影响他们对企业的认知和对企业的参与程度）→→→受众的变化（企业逐渐意识到，其披露文件的受众不再仅仅是人类分析师和投资者）→→→自动化交易的兴起（相当数量的股票买卖是基于机器人和算法处理信息后做出的建议，这些算法依赖于机器学习和自然语言处理技术）。

The annual report (and other regulatory filings) is more than a legal requirement for public companies; it provides an opportunity to communicate financial health, promote culture and brand, and engage with a broad spectrum of stakeholders. How readers process this information significantly affects their perception of and participation in the business. Increasingly, firms recognize that the target audience of their disclosures no longer consists solely of human analysts and investors. A substantial volume of trading is triggered by recommendations from algorithms that process information using machine learning and natural language processing tools.1

Multiple functions of annual reports (beyond legal compliance, annual reports communicate financial health, brand culture, and stakeholder engagement) → Reader perception effects (how readers process information directly affects their perception and participation) → Shifting audience (disclosure audiences now extend beyond human analysts and investors) → Rise of algorithmic trading (a significant share of trading is driven by algorithmic recommendations relying on ML and NLP).
1. 例如，Gara （2018）提到领先的对冲基金The Man Group已开始使用人工智能和算法交易管理其大部分资产（Satariano and Kumar 2017）。 ↩

技术进步和大量信息披露的趋势使这一转变不可避免：$\color{blue}{Cohen\ et\ al.\ (2020 JF)}$ 记录了10-K报告的篇幅从2005年到2017年增加了五倍。那些希望与利益相关者实现预期沟通效果的公司，必须在人工智能时代调整其财务、品牌和预测的表述方式。换句话说，企业应该关注大规模机器学习技术所促成的语言和情感分析的独特逻辑和技术，例如自动化计算过程，这些过程能够在超过人类大脑处理能力的企业披露语料库中，识别正面、负面和中立的意见。虽然现有文献正在逐步跟上并引导投资者运用机器学习和计算工具来提取披露和新闻中的定性信息，但还没有研究探索反馈效应：公司在意识到机器正在"倾听"时如何调整其表述方式。本研究填补了这一空白。

技术进步和信息披露增加→→→沟通的必要调整→→→大规模机器学习的崛起→→→文献滞后于投资实践→→→尚未有研究探讨企业如何调整其表述方式以适应机器读者的需求。

Both technological progress and the sheer volume of disclosures make this trend inevitable: $\color{blue}{Cohen\ et\ al.\ (2020 JF)}$ document that the length of 10-Ks increased fivefold from 2005 to 2017. Firms seeking effective stakeholder communication must adjust how they discuss finances, brands, and forecasts in the age of AI. They should heed the unique logic underlying large-scale machine-learning-based language and sentiment analysis—automated processes that identify positive, negative, and neutral opinions across an entire corpus of firm disclosures beyond human processing capacity. While the literature is catching up with investors' rising aptitude for ML-based qualitative information extraction, no study has explored the feedback effect: how firms adjust their communication knowing that machines are listening. This paper fills this void.

Technological progress and disclosure growth → Necessary communication adjustments → Rise of large-scale ML → Literature lags behind practice → No prior study on how firms adapt disclosure to machine readers.

我们的分析从一个诊断测试开始，该测试连接了公司披露文件的机器友好性（根据$\color{blue}{Allee\ et\ al., (2018JAR)}$的Machine readability测量标准）与公司在EDGAR上提交的SEC文件中预期的机器阅读量。我们为此开发了多个代理变量。第一个变量是机器下载量（Machine downloads），通过追踪大量下载的IP地址来构建。机器请求是机器阅读的前提和必要条件，机器下载的文件数量庞大，这使得它们不可能仅仅由人类读者处理。由于构建Machine downloads的SEC日志文件自2015年起向公众开放，我们的分析隐含假设公司在具体的机器下载数量公开之前已经意识到机器读者的规模。为了放松这一假设，我们还构建了一个基于具有AI能力的机构投资者持股比例的度量，即AI持股量（AI ownership），通过这些投资者的AI相关招聘信息进行跟踪。最后，我们通过计算机构投资者所拥有的加权AI人才供应（AI talent supply）作为代理变量，这是根据投资者总部所在地在特定年份拥有IT学位的适龄劳动人口比例计算的。由于资产管理公司的总部大多在AI时代之前选定，并且与投资组合公司没有直接关系，最后一个变量很可能与解释Machine readability中的遗漏变量正交。

Our analysis begins with a diagnostic test linking how machine-friendly a firm's disclosures are (Machine readability, following $\color{blue}{Allee\ et\ al., (2018JAR)}$) to the expected extent of machine readership for SEC filings on EDGAR, for which we develop multiple proxies. The first, Machine downloads, tracks IP addresses conducting large-batch downloads. Machine requests are a precursor and necessary condition for machine reading, and the volume makes human-only processing implausible. Since the SEC log files used to construct Machine downloads became public in 2015, our analyses assume firms were aware of machine readership before exact download numbers were released. To relax this assumption, we also construct AI ownership, based on share ownership by institutional investors with AI capabilities tracked from AI-related job postings. Finally, we proxy investor technology capacity via AI talent supply—the ownership-weighted proportion of working-age population with IT degrees where investors are headquartered. Because headquarters were mostly chosen before the AI era and bear no direct relation to portfolio firms, this variable is likely orthogonal to omitted variables explaining Machine readability.

我们表明，在公司和年度固定效应的横截面分析中，预期Machine downloads变化一个标准差，与披露文件的Machine readability提高0.24个标准差相关。另一方面，其他（非机器）下载与Machine readability之间没有显著的相关性，这验证了Machine downloads作为机器读者代理变量的有效性。替代变量AI ownership和AI talent supply具有相似的经济和统计显著性。我们进一步验证了主要变量背后的经济机制，显示当Machine downloads较高时，在文件公开后交易速度加快，并且当Machine readability更高时，这一互动效应更加显著。该结果证明了机器处理对信息传播的实际影响。

We show that, in the cross-section with firm and year fixed effects, a one-standard-deviation change in expected Machine downloads is associated with a 0.24-standard-deviation increase in Machine readability. Other (non-machine) downloads bear no meaningful correlation with Machine readability, validating Machine downloads as a proxy for machine readership. The alternative proxies AI ownership and AI talent supply yield similar economic and statistical significance. We further validate the economic mechanism by showing that trades follow more quickly after a filing becomes public when Machine downloads is higher, with an even stronger interactive effect when Machine readability is better. This demonstrates the real impact of machine processing on information dissemination.

在建立了更高的AI读者群体与更具机器友好性的披露文件之间的正相关关系后，我们接下来探讨公司如何管理机器感知到的"情感"和"语调"。已有文献表明，企业披露文件会尝试在与人类读者交流时保持适当的情感和语调，而不会明确欺骗或公然违规（$\color{blue}{Loughran\ and\ McDonald,\ 2011JF}$；$\color{blue}{Kothari\ et\ al.,\ 2009JAR}$）。因此，我们预计公司会采取类似的策略来迎合机器读者。尽管研究人员和实践者长期依赖哈佛心理社会学词典（尤其是Harvard-IV-4 TabNeg文件）来构建人类读者感知的"情感"，$\color{blue}{Loughran\ and\ McDonald,\ (2011JF)}$（以下简称"LM"）的出版为测试我们关于机器读者的假设提供了一个重要事件。这不仅因为该论文提出了一个专门的财务词典，包含积极/消极词汇以及关于前景和不确定性的信息词汇，还因为该论文附带的词汇表已成为行业和学术界用以分类情感的主要词典。时间线和新词典的语境差异使我们能够追踪AI读者群体对企业情感管理的影响。

Having established a positive association between a higher AI reader base and machine-friendlier disclosures, we next explore how firms manage the "sentiment" and "tone" perceived by machines. Corporate disclosures are well documented to strike the right sentiment with human readers without being explicitly dishonest ($\color{blue}{Loughran\ and\ McDonald,\ 2011JF}$; $\color{blue}{Kothari\ et\ al.,\ 2009JAR}$). We expect a similar strategy catering to machine readers. While researchers long relied on the Harvard Psychosociological Dictionary to construct sentiment as perceived by human readers, the publication of $\color{blue}{Loughran\ and\ McDonald,\ (2011JF)}$ ("LM" hereafter) presents an instrumental event for testing our hypothesis—not only because the paper introduced a specialized finance dictionary of positive/negative words but also because its word lists became the leading lexicon for sentiment algorithms in both industry and academia. The differences in timeline and context of the new dictionary allow us to trace the impact of AI readership on corporate sentiment management.

作为第一步，我们确立了那些预期有大量机器下载的公司在2011年之后（即LM词典出版年份）避免使用LM词典中的负面词汇。这样的结构性变化在哈佛词典定义的负面词汇中并不存在。因此，LM – Harvard sentiment与LM sentiment呈相同路径。为了更严格的识别，我们进一步确认了2010年之前高机器下载量公司和低机器下载量公司（前1/3和后1/3分位数）在LM – Harvard sentiment上的平行趋势。2011年后，"高"机器下载量组相较于"低"组，显著减少了使用LM词典中的负面词汇，而不是哈佛词典中的负面词汇。鉴于出版时间的准随机性，情感表达中的双重差分分析更可能归因于公司为了迎合AI读者，而不是现有持续趋势的附带现象。

As a first step, we establish that firms expecting high Machine downloads avoid LM-negative words but only post-2011 (the LM dictionary's publication year). This structural change is absent for words deemed negative by the Harvard dictionary. Consequently, LM – Harvard sentiment follows the same path as LM sentiment. For tighter identification, we confirm a parallel pre-trend in LM – Harvard sentiment between firms with high and low (top and bottom terciles) Machine downloads up to 2010. Post-2011 saw clear divergence: the "high" group significantly reduced, relative to the "low" group, the use of LM-negative words as opposed to Harvard-negative words. Given the quasi-randomness of publication timing, this difference-in-differences in sentiment expression is more likely attributable to firms catering to AI readers than to a preexisting trend.

已记录的关系引发了有趣的均衡含义。如果企业能够在没有成本和约束的情况下通过积极化语言来取悦机器和人类读者，这些信号将很快失去其意义。为了保持一种投资者能够从披露中提取信息的均衡状态，我们假设企业在管理情感和语调时会产生异质的收益和成本。从收益方面来看，面临迫切外部融资需求的企业更有可能抑制使用$\color{blue}{Loughran\ and\ McDonald\ (2011JF)}$负面词汇，并以更便于机器阅读的格式进行披露，以确保正面信号能够被很好地接收。从成本方面来看，面临较高诉讼风险的企业在遣词造句上会更加谨慎。

  • 收益：有外部融资需求的企业更倾向于减少使用负面词汇，并以机器友好的方式披露信息，以确保正面信号传达给投资者。
  • 成本：面临较高诉讼风险的企业在使用措辞上会更加谨慎，避免过度修饰语言。

The documented relation raises intriguing equilibrium implications. If firms could "positify" language without cost to impress machine and human readers, the signals would quickly lose relevance. To maintain an equilibrium in which investors extract information from disclosures, we hypothesize heterogeneous benefits and costs of sentiment management. On the benefit side, firms facing imminent external financing needs are more likely to suppress $\color{blue}{Loughran\ and\ McDonald\ (2011JF)}$ negative words and disclose in more machine-readable formats. On the cost side, firms facing higher litigation risk are more restrained in their word-mincing.

  • Benefits: Firms with external financing needs are more inclined to reduce negative words and adopt machine-friendly disclosure to ensure positive signals reach investors.
  • Costs: Firms facing higher litigation risk exercise greater caution in language, avoiding excessive embellishment.

人工智能技术的快速发展，即便在本文撰写和修订期间，也为我们提供了"样本外"测试。首先，我们利用了由Google在2018年开发的BERT（$\color{blue}{Devlin\ et\ al.,\ 2018}$），这是当前处理文本数据的最先进技术。我们展示了，BERT测量的负面情绪在2018年后对那些具有较高AI读者群体（通过AI ownership和AI talent supply衡量）的公司下降得更多。其次，我们将研究引入了语音场景。早期研究（$\color{blue}{Mayew\ and\ Ventakachalam,\ 2012JF}$）发现，经理人的语音表达经过语音分析软件评估后，可以传达对分析师有价值的增量信息。通过应用软件提取心理学文献中广泛认可的两个情感特征——valence和arousal（对应于声音的积极性和兴奋度）——从公司电话会议中的管理层发言中，我们发现预期拥有更多机器读者的公司，管理层表现出更多的语音积极性和兴奋度。这与越来越多的经理人为提高其在可量化指标上的语音表现而寻求专业帮助的趋势相呼应。

The rapid evolution of AI technology, even during the writing of this paper, provides "out-of-sample" tests. First, we leverage BERT ($\color{blue}{Devlin\ et\ al.,\ 2018}$), the state-of-the-art for machine processing of textual data. We show that BERT-measured negative sentiment drops more post-2018 for firms with higher AI readership, measured by AI ownership and AI talent supply. Second, we extend the study into the speech setting. Earlier work ($\color{blue}{Mayew\ and\ Ventakachalam,\ 2012JF}$) found that managers' vocal expressions, assessed by vocal analytic software, convey incremental information valuable to analysts. Applying software to extract valence and arousal (positivity and excitedness of voice) from managerial speeches in conference calls, we find that managers of firms with higher expected machine readership exhibit more positivity and excitement in vocal tones—echoing the trend of managers increasingly seeking professional help to improve vocal performance on quantifiable metrics.

我们的研究基于不断扩展的文献，这些文献探讨了通过SEC文件下载进行信息获取和传播的方式（$\color{blue}{Bernard\ et\ al.,\ 2020}$；$\color{blue}{Chen\ et\ al.,\ 2020}$；Cao et al. 2021；Crane, Crotty, and Umar 2022），并从一个新的角度探讨了机器处理的后果及人类的反应。快速增长的文本分析文献的核心主题是，披露文件中的定性信息和书写质量能够预测资产回报和企业绩效。随着现代机器学习技术的进步，计算文本分析逐渐扩展到非文本数据领域，如公司电话会议的音频和创业公司演示的录像。我们的研究不同于现有文献，因为我们探讨了管理层在应对AI分析工具日益广泛应用时的披露策略。

Our study builds on an expanding literature on information acquisition and dissemination via SEC-filing downloads ($\color{blue}{Bernard\ et\ al.,\ 2020}$; $\color{blue}{Chen\ et\ al.,\ 2020}$; Cao et al. 2021; Crane, Crotty, and Umar 2022), taking a new angle on the consequences of and human reactions to machine processing. A central theme from the growing textual analysis literature is that qualitative information and writing quality of disclosures predict asset returns and corporate performance. Computational textual analyses have been steadily advanced by modern ML techniques and extended to non-text data such as conference call audio and startup pitch videos. Our study departs from extant literature by exploring managerial disclosure strategies in response to the growing presence of AI analytical tools in both industry and academia.
